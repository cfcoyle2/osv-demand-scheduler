## Sparta EV Run Options: Slide-Ready Pros and Cons

| Option | Pros | Cons | Overall Takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Current State | Established operating rhythm. Existing vessels and EV timing are already understood by the team. Preserves current recovery buffer for the existing asset base. | Does not account for Sparta demand. Not a workable future-state schedule on its own. | Useful baseline for comparison, not a solution. |
| Option 1 | Cleanest and most stable operating model. Sparta demand is isolated onto a dedicated OSV. Only Mars requires an EV date change. Lower schedule congestion and better recovery after delays or weather. | Requires adding an OSV. Highest operating cost. Works against the objective of solving the problem with the existing fleet. | Best reliability, worst cost efficiency. |
| Option 2 | Keeps existing EV schedules unchanged. Simplifies stakeholder messaging because no asset date changes are required. | Too many FSVs committed at the same time. Highest fleet saturation. Leaves limited recovery flexibility when disruptions occur. Team already views it as non-viable. | Not recommended for execution. |
| Option 3 | Best cost/operability balance for most of the year. Avoids adding a full-time OSV. Keeps overall vessel count closer to current state. Supports Sparta while using alternating FSV and OSV coverage at Auger and ESA. | Tighter recovery margin. Higher exposure to weather disruption and fronts. Harder to recover once slips begin, especially later in the year. | Best base strategy if paired with clear contingency triggers. |

### Recommended Slide Message
Use Option 3 as the base strategy during stable months because it is the lowest-cost workable solution. Maintain Option 1 as the contingency for fronts season or repeated recovery failures. Do not advance Option 2.
