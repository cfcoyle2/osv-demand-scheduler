## Executive Summary

Based on cargo volume analysis and operational timing, **4-5 dedicated OSVs plus 1 FSV** are recommended to support the Sparta 20K well operations, with a primary focus on 3-4 OSVs during peak periods and weekly FSV runs for consumables and urgent supplies.

## Fast Supply Vessel (FSV) Operations

### FSV-1 Specifications & Role
**Vessel Type:** Fast Supply Vessel (FSV)  
**Primary Mission:** Weekly consumables and urgent supply runs  
**Capacity:** 2,500 sq ft deck + 500 bbls liquid capacity  
**Transit Speed:** Higher speed than standard OSV (24-28 hours vs 36 hours)  
**Loading Time:** 48 hours (vs 72 hours for OSV)  
**Offloading Time:** 6-8 hours (vs 12-18 hours for OSV)  
**Total Cycle Time:** 4-5 days (vs 6-7 days for OSV)

### Weekly FSV Cargo Manifest
**Typical FSV Load (Weekly):**
- Fresh water and potable supplies
- Drilling chemicals and additives (small volumes)
- Spare parts and consumables
- Food and galley supplies
- Safety equipment and PPE
- Mail and small packages
- Personnel transfers (if equipped)
- Urgent/emergency supplies

### FSV Schedule Benefits
1. **Frequency:** Weekly runs maintain steady supply chain
2. **Speed:** Faster transit times reduce emergency response time
3. **Flexibility:** Can adjust schedule for urgent needs
4. **Efficiency:** Smaller, faster vessel more cost-effective for consumables
5. **Reliability:** Reduces burden on larger OSVs for small cargo
6. **Personnel:** Can facilitate crew changes if equipped

## Recommendations

### Optimal OSV Configuration: 4 Primary + 1 Standby + 1 FSV

**Primary Fleet (4 OSVs):**
- 2 vessels for bulk fluids (SLB/Tetra rotation)
- 1 vessel for drill pipe and heavy equipment
- 1 vessel for heavy cargo and mixed loads

**Standby Vessel (1 OSV):**
- Active during overlap period (Jan 1-24)
- Weather/breakdown backup
- Peak demand support

**Fast Supply Vessel (1 FSV):**
- Weekly runs throughout program (12 total trips)
- Consumables: food, water, chemicals, spare parts
- Shorter loading time (24-48 hours vs 72 hours)
- Faster transit (24-28 hours vs 36 hours)
- Personnel transfer capability
- Urgent supply response

### Contingency Planning
- Contract 1 additional spot vessel for emergency backup
- Negotiate priority berthing at Fourchon for faster turnaround
- Pre-stage critical fluids at port 1 week ahead of schedule
- Maintain daily communication with rig for consumption updates

### Cost Optimization Strategies
1. Negotiate term rates for 80+ day commitment
2. Consolidate loads to minimize trips
3. Back-haul empty containers/waste to improve vessel utilization
4. Share vessel capacity with nearby rigs if available
