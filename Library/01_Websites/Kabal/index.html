What is Kabal?

Kabal is a cloud-based logistics platform used across Shell's Gulf of America (GoA) operations to manage cargo movements between suppliers and offshore facilities. It enables supply chain partners to plan and execute logistics operations by providing visibility into upcoming cargo demand and helping Shell optimize marine resources.

The Kabal workflow relies on collaboration between Shell, suppliers, logistics contractors, and marine teams to ensure timely and efficient cargo handling from source to offshore destination.

Planning Horizon Phase

Spans 3 months or more to 21 days before the Base Delivery Date (BDD). This is when demand is identified and logistics structures are built in Kabal.

3 Months+ to 21 Days Before BDD

🎯 Purpose of This Phase

Sources: SC Delivery – Future Logistics Optimization, Marine Mapping Process

The Planning Horizon Phase is when the Area Logistics Coordinator (ALC/SCLC) engages business stakeholders to understand future cargo demand. The goal is to ensure accurate offshore demand is entered early, enabling a reliable schedule for the Control Tower and C-Logistics.

Success in this phase means fewer last-minute changes, better visibility, and meeting business needs with the leanest possible marine and aviation fleet.

📅 3 Months+ to 30 Days Before BDD

  • SCLC engages business stakeholders to identify future logistics needs
  • Wells demand informed by rig sequence and Well Operations Team conversations
  • Production, MTAC, TAR & Projects demand via IAP Logistics Flag meetings and direct functional leader communication
  • Historical demand averages pulled from Archetype activities to understand demand profile for Capacity Plan
  • Future demand will eventually be captured within Kabal

📅 30 to 21 Days Before BDD

  • Well Operations Team and OLCs build Time Planners in Kabal
  • Group Loads are created from the Time Planners
  • Cargo Packages are created within Group Loads
  • Each Cargo Package placed in the correct Group Load (1 run before activity starts)

👥 Demand Signal by Business Type

Source: Marine Mapping Process with New WoW
  • Production EV Runs: Functional Leads / IAP Focal Points attend IAP meeting weekly and flag upcoming projects with material movement
  • Projects: Projects Engineer
  • Major Maintenance: MM Block Coordinator
  • Campaigns: MM Block Coordinator
  • Routine Maintenance: Onshore Maintenance Lead (OMC)
  • TAR/Pit Stop: MM Block Coordinator
  • Wells – Bulk (Mud, Brine, Cement): Well Engineer, Fluid/Mud Engineer, Cementer
  • Wells – Vendor Equipment: Well Engineer selects vendor/supplier
  • Wells – Tangibles (casing, wellheads): Well/Subsea Engineers
  • Swire Containers / CCUs: Crane Crew
  • Rig Supplies: Rig Owners (Noble, Transocean)

🔄 Control Tower Model

The Control Tower model separates planning and execution to enable SCLC to focus forward:

Control Tower Execution
Manages marine resource allocation and executes the daily plan. Makes fast, high-quality resource decisions.
Logistics Coordinators Planning
Focus on identifying future resource needs beyond 7 days, consolidating demand into fewer voyages, building the marine schedule.

Scheduling Horizon Phase

The 21 to 4 days window before the Base Delivery Date. Suppliers play a pivotal role — finalizing cargo footprints, fluid types and quantities, and container details.

21 Days → 4 Days Before BDD

📧 21–14 Days: Supplier Notification & Info Entry

  • Supplier receives automated Kabal email notification with a direct cargo package link
  • Supplier opens the cargo package and completes the Supplier Information Section
  • Required fields: Supplier Organizes Transport (Yes/No), Supplier Ready Date, Container/Basket Footprint Estimates, Lifts count
  • Supplier updates Delivery Info Section: Pickup Status (Ready/Not Ready), From Warehouse location, Contact Information
  • Control Tower Marine Focal begins scheduling routes to MEL Group Loads

📦 14–4 Days: Add Cargo Items

  • Supplier adds cargo line items — manually or via Excel template upload (recommended)
  • All rental items require individual lines with unique serial numbers for equipment tracking
  • Bulk chemicals: enter total quantity (barrels, gallons, or cubic feet) with specific gravity and fluid type
  • Key required fields: Delivered quantity, Type, Item Description, Serial No., Proc (Purchased/Stock/Consignment/Free/Rental), DG flag
  • Upload certifications, photos, BOLs in the Files section — no upload limit
  • For loose lifts (individual items not in CCUs): enter Length, Width, Height, Weight in the Loose field

📐 7–4 Days: Add CCUs & Pack Cargo

  • Supplier adds actual containers (CCUs) from the preloaded Swire CCU catalog
  • Enter CCU tag/serial number — smart search auto-fills dimensions and weight
  • Pack cargo items into CCUs using drag-and-drop within the cargo package
  • For chemicals split across multiple totes, enter the portion quantity per container
  • Try to order containers within 7 days of BDD to reduce rental costs
  • Cargo Package approved by Asset Logistics Focal Point or OLC between 7–4 days before departure

🎨 Supplier Layout Color Codes

Source: Kabal – Suppliers Guide for Offshore Deliveries REV1

Shell's Supplier Layout highlights cargo packages needing attention. To access it: click Layout Reports → Supplier Layout.

⚠️ Yellow – Square footage estimates not entered at least 14 days before BDD (see Supplier Info section)
🔴 Red – Correct warehouse in Delivery Info Section not chosen, or Cargo Package still Open within 4 days of BDD
✅ Green – Cargo Package complete and Approved by Shell

📅 Finding Cargo Packages in Kabal

  • Navigate to the Loadout Request Page
  • Default date filter shows only 7 days — adjust to 3 weeks ahead to see all upcoming packages
  • Click the Date Range filter → select today as start date → select 3 weeks ahead as end date → click Apply

Execution Phase

The final 4 days before departure. Cargo is closed out, approved, dock appointments are set, trucking is arranged, and the manifest is locked.

4 Days Before BDD → Vessel Departure

✅ Step 1: Close Out the Cargo Package (4 Days Before BDD)

  • Supplier reviews all cargo package fields for accuracy
  • Clicks "Close Out" to finalize the package
  • Clicks "Send Email" to notify the Shell distribution list
  • Shell Logistics reviews and approves the package ~3 days before shipment

🏗️ Step 2: Dock Appointment Scheduling

  • C-Port Expeditor creates and communicates the 72-hour Dock Appointment Schedule
  • 4 places to view your Dock Appointment:
    1. Within the Cargo Package – Operator Request Delivery Field
    2. Within the Transport Order Tab – Latest Delivery Date
    3. In the Transport Order Email – body of the email
    4. In the Transport Order PDF attachment
  • Requires completed and approved Cargo Packages at least 3 days before BDD
  • Requires complete and accurate Project/Activity fields, weights, dimensions, and CCU info

🚛 Step 3: Trucking & Cargo to Port

  • If Shell provides trucking: Ryder contacts supplier to confirm pickup arrangements
  • If Supplier provides trucking: Supplier delivers cargo to port at the scheduled appointment time
  • Cargo received at C-Port 2 and staged for loading

🔒 Step 4: Manifest Locked (72 Hours Before Departure)

  • C-Logistics Load Planner creates the final voyage manifest
  • All materials move to port at their specific dock appointment time
  • Manifest is considered locked 72 hours prior to vessel departure
  • No changes to the manifest after this point without authorization

📋 Terminal Delivery Framework

Source: Kabal – Suppliers Guide – Dock Appointments

Shell and ECO have moved from a time-and-materials model to a service level and performance-based approach at C-Port 2. This Terminal Delivery Framework:

  • Improves truck turnaround time
  • Reduces lifts and exposure
  • Strengthens end-to-end planning capability
  • Requires scheduled delivery appointments for most materials
  • ECO uses Kabal data to plan vessel manifests, schedule appointments, and optimize terminal operations

⚓ Vessel Loading

  • Cargo loaded onto vessel according to finalized manifest
  • C-Logistics Load Planner confirms all manifested items are on board
  • Vessel departs C-Port 2 for offshore assets per scheduled route

📍 In-Transit Tracking

  • Vessel tracked via Kabal and Ryder Share platform during transit
  • OLC and Deck Supervisors review manifest and provide updates
  • Road Planner and Vessel notified of any last instructions
  • C-Log updates load plan per rig instructions during transit window

🛢️ Offshore Receipt

  • Vessel arrives at offshore asset (rig, TLP, FPSO, spar)
  • Crane crew offloads cargo and verifies against offload sheet
  • Each package placed in general staging area first
  • Forklift moves materials to designated area (vendor area, waste, Ryder pickup, etc.)

  • Kabal – primary cargo and vessel tracking platform
  • Ryder Share – rydershare.com/main – trucking and logistics visibility

📥 Step 1: Backhaul Identification

  • OLC and Deck Supervisor review if a backhaul Material Return (MR) is needed
  • Backhaul inputted into Kabal when confirmed
  • Preliminary manifest created and sent to OLC for approval
  • OLC validates the manifest before departure and sends to C-Logistics for review

🚢 Step 2: Vessel Arrives & Dock Coordinator Verifies

  • Inbound Dock Coordinator walks the back deck of the returning vessel
  • Confirms each item on the vessel matches records in Kabal
  • Crane crew verifies through offload sheet and moves each package to general staging

🏭 Step 3: Staging Areas

Forklift picks up each material and places it in designated area:

  • 🏢 Vendor/Supplier Area – rental equipment returning to supplier
  • ♻️ Waste/Trash Area – materials for disposal
  • 🧪 Environmental Staging – chemicals, hazardous materials
  • 🚿 Tank Cleaning – tanks requiring cleaning before reuse
  • ⚓ Vessel Standby – materials staying at base for next run

📦 Step 4: Vendor Contact & Pickup

  • Inbound Dock Coordinator contacts vendors for pickup
  • Vendor or Ryder receives the Material Return (MR)
  • Materials transported to vendor facility
  • Vendor confirms receipt — Kabal records updated and backhaul cycle closed out

User Access & Setup

How to create a Kabal account, request a Shell GoA role, and subscribe to email notification lists.

🔐 Creating a Kabal Account

Source: Kabal_Shell GoA – New User Access Guide (October 2025)
  1. Go to https://kabal.com
  2. Click "Login" then select "Kabal"
  3. Click "Sign up for Kabal"
  4. Enter your company email address (not a private email — approved domains only)
  5. Select Work Location (country of the operator) and Work for (Company)
  6. Click "Request New User Account"
  7. Enter the authorization code sent to your email and click "Confirm Account"
  8. Set your account password
  9. Read and accept the Kabal Privacy Policy (required on first login)

🔑 Requesting a Shell GoA User Role

  1. Search for the application "Shell GoA"
  2. Click "Shell GoA – Operator (US)"
  3. Click "Apply for an Application Role"
  4. Fill out the request form — be specific and clear about your purpose
  5. Submit Role Request for Approval

📝 What to Include in Your Role Request

  • Purpose: e.g., "I will be sending equipment offshore to Shell Assets for Company X"
  • For suppliers — specify Parent Company, Sub Company, and address (e.g., Halliburton US – WellDynamics, New Iberia, LA)
  • Indicate if Kabal Training Guides are needed from a Shell Super User
  • State if you want to be included in your Company's Kabal Email List

📧 Subscribing to Email Notification Lists

Source: Kabal – Get Added to Email Lists
  1. From the Home Page, click the Settings icon in the Navigation Pane (left side)
  2. Expand the Application dropdown
  3. Click "Email List"
  4. Select your list from the Email List dropdown
  5. Select Constraint → Company to filter to your company
  6. Add your email address and click the green "Add New" button
📬 Loadout Request List – Receive emails for offshore delivery requests
📬 Manifests Email List – Receive emails when items are on outbound/inbound manifests
📬 Redelivery Manifest – Receive emails for items shipped from port back to vendor yards
📬 Warehouse Order List – Receive emails for vendor-to-vendor movements
📬 Transport Order List – Receive emails regarding trucking information

Group Loads & Cargo Packages

How Shell Logistics Coordinators and OLCs create Group Loads, set up Cargo Packages, and track status through the IAP dashboard.

📦 Creating Group Loads

Source: Step by Step – Creating Group Loads and Cargo Packages in Kabal
  1. Navigate to Kabal → Cargo Tab → Activity Planning
  2. Select your Rig/Asset
  3. Click "Add New" to add a group load for the remainder of the year
  4. Click into the overall group load to add each weekly EV (every voyage) run
  5. For each run, select: Base Delivery Date, Offshore Req. Date, Base Port, Account Code

💰 Entering Charge / Cost Codes

  1. Under "Account Code", enter the marine cost code provided by the Deck Supervisor / OLC / Foreman
  2. If a new cost code is needed, follow the Cost Codes guide (see Cost Codes section)

📊 Reading the IAP Plan

  1. Under the IAP Tab, view the "Activity List View" and filter to your asset
  2. Sort activity Start Date in chronological order
  3. Filter activities that have material movement — either flagged activities or those confirmed with your functional lead in the IAP meeting
  4. Copy the ID number to paste into the Kabal cargo package

📋 Inputting a Cargo Package in Kabal

  1. Go to Cargo → Equipment List → Select Asset/Rig
  2. Open a new cargo package into the proper Group Load / run (1 run before the activity starts offshore)
  3. Enter the Supplier Name
  4. Paste the Cargo Package number from IAP Activity List, prefix it with #IAP
  5. Click "Add New Cargo Package"
  6. Add any other known information (basket estimates, quantities, etc.)

📨 Sending Cargo Package to Supplier

  1. Under Actions, click "Send Email"
  2. Confirm the correct supplier contact is listed on the email distribution
  3. Ask your functional lead for the correct supplier contact if unsure

📍 Tracking Cargo Package Status in IAP

Use the IAP with Logistics View to track cargo package readiness against offshore activity start dates. Click the Cargo Package ID in IAP to jump directly to the package in Kabal.

  • IAP Readiness Color — changes based on whether the activity date changes relative to cargo readiness
  • Update the manifest date in Kabal manually as needed

Cargo Items & CCUs

Detailed guide for adding cargo line items (manually or via Excel) and packing them into Cargo Carrying Units (CCUs).

📌 What Is a Cargo Item?

Source: Kabal – Suppliers Guide for Offshore Deliveries REV1

A Cargo Item is any equipment or material either packed into a container or shipped as a loose lift or bulk material. Examples include:

  • Tools, temporary piping, drill string crossovers
  • Pails or sacks of chemicals
  • Loose lifts rated for offshore dynamic lifting (filter presses, pumps, tool houses, IWOCS reels, coil tubing reels)
  • Total quantity of a chemical in tote tanks

Cargo line-item details are required for all rental items in baskets to enable virtual tracking in Kabal.

✏️ Adding Cargo Items Manually

  1. Under Cargo Items section, click "Add New Item"
  2. Complete all required (highlighted) fields:
Delivered – Quantity of the line item
Type – Select from picklist ("Item, Other" acceptable for most items)
Item Description – Thread type, casing weight/type, etc.
Serial No. – One line per unique serial number
Proc – Purchased / Stock / Consignment / Free / Rental
DG – Check if Dangerous Goods
Loose – Length, Width, Height, Weight for individual lifts
Files – Certifications, photos, BOLs (no limit)

📊 Adding Cargo Items via Excel (Recommended)

  1. From the Kabal Home Page, download the Cargo Package Upload Template
  2. Complete the yellow-shaded Material Details section
  3. Save the completed Excel file
  4. In Kabal, click the dropdown arrow next to "Add New Item" → select "Add from Excel"
  5. Drag and drop the Excel file into the Auto Import area
  6. Kabal returns to the Cargo Package with items populated
  7. Note: Dimensions are not imported from Excel — add Loose dimensions manually for rental items and individual lifts

💡 Excel Entry Tips

  • Use quantity >1 only for items that don't need individual tracking (e.g., consumables)
  • List rental items individually — one line per serial number
  • For chemicals: enter total quantity as one line (e.g., 3,000 gallons of MEG)
  • Use the correct Value Unit for each item
  • Add containers directly in Kabal (not via Excel) — Swire CCUs have pre-filled weight/dimensions

📐 Adding CCUs (Cargo Carrying Units)

  1. Click the green "Add New Unit" button in the cargo package
  2. Select from the first 10 options in the dropdown (CCUs only — do not select Packing Units)
  3. In the Tag Number field, type the CCU serial number — smart search suggests matching CCUs from Kabal master data
  4. If adding a new CCU not in the system: click "Add [name]" → click Edit Status → provide reason "Container does not exist" → fill mandatory fields → add certifications → click the orange "Open – Click to Close Out" button

🤝 Packing Cargo Items into CCUs

  1. Click and hold the hand icon next to the cargo line item
  2. Drag and drop the item into the appropriate CCU
  3. For multiple items: hold Ctrl to select additional items (they turn green), then drag all at once
  4. For bulk items split across containers: enter the portion quantity in the popup and click "Pack"
  5. Confirm the Container column on the line item shows the correct CCU

Vendor-to-Vendor Movements

For Shell-owned equipment moving between vendors, or Swire container movements — not direct offshore delivery.

📌 What Is a Vendor-to-Vendor Movement?

Source: Kabal – Vendor to Vendor Movements (February 2025)

Covers two scenarios:

  1. Shell-owned equipment requiring movement from one vendor to another vendor or to the Houma DC
  2. Swire container movements — containerizing cargo going offshore when the contract doesn't include supplier-provided containers, or returning empty containers back to Swire

The supplier shipping the cargo is responsible for completing the Kabal Warehouse Order. This process does not include cargo going directly to port for offshore delivery.

📋 Creating a Warehouse Order

  1. From the Kabal Home Page, add a Warehouse Orders favorite:
    • Click the Star icon → search "Warehouse" → select "Warehouse/Orders" → click Save
  2. On the Warehouse Order Home Page, use the Warehouse Filter to select the warehouse the cargo is shipping from
  3. Click the green "Add WH Order" button
  4. Select "Add New Item Package"

📝 Completing the Warehouse Order Info Section

Mandatory Fields:

  • Est. Delivery Date – Pick a date giving Ryder at least 48 hours to coordinate
  • Order Name – Unique description of the cargo being moved
  • Warehouse – The destination of the cargo

Optional Fields: PO Number, Workorder Number, MMR No., Remarks

📦 Completing the Order Items Section

  1. Click the "Add New Item" button
  2. Fill out the Order Item fields for each cargo item
  3. For items not containerized (drill pipe, equipment for dynamic lifting, etc.) — completing the Loose field is Mandatory
  4. Click the Loose Icon to open the shipping info box:
    • Unit Weight, Lifts# (usually 1), Total Length, Total Width, Total Height
    • Close the popup — information saves automatically

Dock Appointments

Requirements and procedures for scheduled cargo delivery appointments at C-Port 2 under the Terminal Delivery Framework.

🏗️ Terminal Delivery Framework Overview

Source: Kabal – Suppliers Guide – Dock Appointments (June 2025)

C-Port 2 now requires scheduled delivery appointments for most materials. This is part of Shell's performance-based contract with ECO for terminal services at Port Fourchon.

Key Requirements:

  • Completed and approved Kabal Cargo Packages at least 3 days before the Base Delivery Date
  • Complete and accurate Project/Activity (Group Load) fields
  • Full shipping details: weights, dimensions, CCU information
  • ECO uses this data to plan vessel manifests, schedule appointments, and optimize terminal operations

📍 Where to View Your Dock Appointment Time

There are 4 places to find your dock appointment:

1️⃣ Within the Cargo Package

The Dock Appointment Time is displayed in the Operator Request Delivery Field of the cargo package.

2️⃣ Within the Transport Order

On an approved Cargo Package, click the Transport Order Tab. The Dock Appointment Time is the Latest Delivery Date.

3️⃣ In the Transport Order Email

The Dock Appointment Time is included in the body of the Transport Order email sent automatically from Kabal.

4️⃣ In the Transport Order PDF

The Dock Appointment Time is also contained in the Transport Order PDF attachment.

Cost Codes

How to create and manage project cost codes in Kabal to ensure correct financial charging for cargo movements.

💰 Creating a New Cost Code

Source: Kabal – Entering Cost Codes (February 2025)
  1. Navigate to Settings → Application → Cost Codes
  2. Use the search bar to verify whether the cost code already exists before creating a new one
  3. If no results found, click the green "Add New Cost Code" button
  4. Populate all required fields:
    • Code Name / Project Name – The name displayed in Kabal
    • Cost Code / Project Code – Copy and paste the exact cost code
    • Rig/Location – Choose from the picklist
    • Active Dates – Start and end dates. If no end date in IAP, use 12/31 of the current year
  5. Click the orange Activate button to make the cost code available
  6. The new cost code is now available to select in Kabal cargo packages

📌 When Are Cost Codes Needed?

  • When creating Group Loads for upcoming production projects or campaigns
  • For MTAC and TAR activities requiring dedicated cost tracking
  • Obtained from: Deck Supervisor, OLC, or Foreman
  • Source of truth for new cost codes: the IAP Activity Dashboard

Glossary

Key terms and abbreviations used in Kabal and Shell GoA logistics operations.

BDD – Base Delivery Date
The estimated date a scheduled vessel run is planned to depart C-Port 2 for offshore assets. Drives all planning KPIs and scheduling windows.
CCU – Cargo Carrying Unit
Any container used to carry cargo offshore — baskets, boxes, totes, tank containers. Shell uses Swire CCUs. Each has a unique tag/serial number.
MEL – Marine Equipment List / Group Load
A Group Load in Kabal that represents a planned future voyage. Cargo Packages are assigned to Group Loads which feed route planning.
CP – Cargo Package
A Kabal record representing a single shipment from one supplier to one offshore asset. Contains supplier info, cargo items, CCUs, and logistics details.
SCLC – Shell Logistics Coordinator
The primary planning-focused logistics role. Responsible for demand identification, Group Load creation, and supplier engagement beyond 7 days.
OLC – Offshore Logistics Coordinator
Asset-level logistics coordinator. Builds Time Planners, creates Cargo Packages, approves packages, and validates manifests.
ALC – Area Logistics Coordinator
Predecessor to the SCLC role. Managed all logistics for a cluster of assets. Now separated into SCLC (planning) and Control Tower (execution).
IAP – Integrated Activity Planning
Shell's activity planning system. Used to flag upcoming projects with material movement. Cargo Package IDs are linked to IAP activity numbers.
MFP – Marine Focal Point
Control Tower role responsible for creating vessel routes, allocating Cargo Packages to voyages, and managing the marine schedule.
BOL – Bill of Lading
Shipping document that details the type, quantity, and destination of cargo. Uploaded to Cargo Package Files section in Kabal.
DG – Dangerous Goods
Cargo classified as hazardous under offshore transport regulations. Must be flagged on the Cargo Item in Kabal.
SDS – Safety Data Sheet
Required documentation for chemical/fluid cargo items. Uploaded to the Files section of the cargo item in Kabal.
MR – Material Return
A backhaul order in Kabal. Issued when offshore equipment is being returned to a vendor or the Houma DC.
MTAC – Maintenance, Technical, and Campaigns
A category of offshore work requiring logistics support. Demand flagged through IAP meetings with MM Block Coordinators.
TAR – Turnaround
A planned maintenance shutdown of a facility. Significant logistics demand event. Demand flagged by MM Block Coordinator.
GoA – Gulf of America
The operational geography for Shell's offshore assets.
C-Port 2
Shell's primary supply base port in Port Fourchon, Louisiana. Primary departure point for all offshore cargo. Operated by ECO under a performance-based contract.
Houma DC
Shell's distribution center in Houma, Louisiana. Used for staging, storage, and vendor-to-vendor movements before cargo goes to port.
NPT – Non-Productive Time
Time lost on offshore operations due to logistics delays. Reducing NPT is a key driver of the Kabal and Control Tower logistics model.
Proc – Procurement Category
Field on cargo items indicating ownership: Purchased, Stock, Consignment, Free, or Rental. Rental items require individual serial number entries.
EV Run – Efficient/Value Run
A scheduled recurring vessel run to a specific offshore asset. EV runs are set up as individual Group Load entries in Kabal for each planned departure.
